Black Bean and Sweet Potato Curry

Description

Ingredients

  • 2 sweet potatoes, scrubbed and cubed.
  • ½ Butternut squash, cut into 1 cm cubes.
  • 1½ tsp each:
    • ground cumin
    • ground coriander
    • turmeric
    • sweet paprika
    • garam masala
  • 2 tbsp light olive or rapeseed oil
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2-3 garlic cloves, crushed
  • 1 tsp fresh grated ginger
  • 14 oz can chopped tomatoes
  • 1 tbsp tomato puree
  • 9 fl oz hot water
  • 2 14 oz cans black beans, drained and rinsed
  • 7 oz baby spinach or pak choi, roughly chopped.
  • A handful of fresh coriander, chopped
  • Juice of 1/2 lemon
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • Plain greek yogurt

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 350°F. Place the prepared sweet potatoes and butternut squash on a baking tray, sprinkle over a little salt, and ½ teaspoon each of cumin and paprika. Add 1 tbsp of oil and toss so all vegetables are well coated. Roast in the oven for 20-25 minutes.
  2. Meanwhile, heat remaining oil in a large pan over low to moderate heat. Add the onion and fry for 3-4 minutes until softened.
  3. Add garlic, ginger, and remaining spices to onions. Cook for one minute.
  4. Add the canned tomatoes, tomato purée, hot water, and beans. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for 10 minutes.
  5. Add the roasted and baby spinach/pak choi and turn off the heat (the spinach will wilt down).
  6. Stir in the fresh coriander (reserve a little to serve), lemon juice, and cashews and season to taste.
  7. Serve with the remaining coriander and greek yogurt.
